## Challenge Tracks

All submissions must align with one of the three challenge tracks. The tracks define the theme of impact, not the full scope of the idea, so teams are free to build any solution that fits one of these areas.

- Agents Track.
- AutoHDR Photo-to-Video Track.
- BrainForge and Vicinity Texas Open Data Track.

Teams should check the Hackathon Notion for the unique requirements of each track.

## Bounties

### Build with Miro

Build an application for Miro, use Miro AI features, or integrate with Miro. The project can target Miro's Marketplace, the Miroverse, or be an excellent demo to share.

- Prize: $500 cash.

### Most Patentable Hack

Build technology that is unique and patentable. To qualify, teams must submit their patent through Deepinvent. The Deepinvent team will review patent submissions in the platform and select the winner.

- Prize: free provisional patent filing through Deepinvent plus $500 cash.

### Top Science Project

Build technology related to scientific innovation. Eligible areas include cancer research, materials science, genetics, peptide discovery, AI research, and other science-related projects.

- Prize: $250,000 Deepinvent license for one year, including godmode access.

### AutoHDR Photo-to-Video Track

The top three submissions for the Photo-to-Video track receive additional prize money.

- 1st place: $5,000.
- 2nd place: $2,500.

## Project Submission

The judging philosophy focuses on systems engineering and innovation. A winning project should be a functioning system that produces novel and valuable work output, not only a slide deck or a simple API wrapper.

### Judging Criteria

Projects are judged on a 100-point scale:

- Technical execution and completeness: Did the team build a working, complex system?
- Partner ecosystem and utility: Did the team leverage the unique tools provided?
- Value and impact: Is the solution useful and valuable in the real world?
- Innovation and execution: Did the team push the boundaries of what is possible with the technology?

### Submission Checklist

Submissions are due by 11:00 AM on Sunday. The extracted OCR mentions "Sunday, April 12th" in one page, while the Hack Fair section mentions "Sunday, May 10th"; verify the official date in the Hackathon Notion.

Each submission must include:

- Project description.
- 3-5 minute Loom video demo.

Submission may take 15-20 minutes, so teams should plan accordingly and record the demo video as early as possible.

## FAQ

Yes, the event provides a lot of caffeine.

The team will not be eligible to win. Since submission can take 15 or more minutes, teams should submit early.

### Can a team submit for multiple tracks?

Teams may submit for up to two tracks, but the event advice is to focus on one strong submission. Top submissions may be eligible to be featured by partners.

## Codex Agentic Loop

The event materials describe a Codex agentic loop:

- It starts with a prompt: give the agent initial direction, identify which files to read, and state the goals.
- The agent calls tools: it can read code, search company knowledge, write files, and loop until task completion.
- The conversation history builds context: every action taken and every file read adds to the model context.
